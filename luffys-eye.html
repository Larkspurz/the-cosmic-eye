Luffy’s Eye: The Unthinking Gaze of the Free Spirit

When I look at the world through Luffy’s eyes... everything becomes simple.

No judgment.
No comparison.
No analysis.

Just things as they are.

Most people see something — and a thousand thoughts start moving.
"Is this good? Is this bad? Should I fear this? Can I use this? Will this hurt me? Is this useful?"
The mind floods itself.

But Luffy’s Eye does none of this.

He sees.
And that’s all.

A man with food is food.
A man with a sword is a sword.
A friend is a friend.
An enemy is just someone to punch away.

No past, no future — just this moment.
Like a child.
Like an animal.
Like the pure Eye — the one that only watches without weight.

But Luffy’s Eye is not cold like a god’s.
It burns with freedom.
He acts — simple, straight, without second thought.
No inner debate.
No struggle of sides.

That’s why his rage is pure. His joy is pure. His sadness is pure.
Because nothing is mixed.
No calculation, no lie.

In a way, Luffy is the small, playful version of the Cosmic Eye — the Eye that laughs, eats meat, and stretches across the sky.
Detached... yet fully alive.

And maybe... that’s why the world follows him.

Because in his gaze — people feel what they forgot:
Life without weight. Life without the noise. Just life — as it is.
